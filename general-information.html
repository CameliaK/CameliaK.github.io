Performance objectives

Performance objectives
By the end of the course, students will be able to:
  • Define and explain key concepts in discourse analysis.
  • Differentiate between spoken and written discourse, as well as identifying structural and functional differences.
  • Analyze distinct types of discourse using appropriate analytical tools.
  • Apply discourse analysis techniques to educational and professional contexts.
  • Reflect on the role of discourse in language teaching and learning.

Rationale

Rationale

As future English teachers, students will understand how language functions beyond the sentence level to help learners develop their communicative competence. This course provides theoretical and practical knowledge to analyze written and spoken discourse in different contexts. Therefore, students are provided with essential skills for language teaching, curriculum design, and assessment, based on discourse. By exploring discourse, students will also become more aware of the social and cultural dimensions of language.

Methodology

Methodology

The course contains both theoretical and practical activities, designed according to student-centered principles so that the students can practice analyzing discourse in multiple ways. Students will participate in portfolio activities and exchange information and tips aimed at bridging the gap between Discourse Analysis (DA) and English Language Teaching (ELT) practice. The final project aims to consolidate everything students learn in a comprehensive lesson plan designed with DA principles in mind. All activities uploaded will be returned with formative feedback.
