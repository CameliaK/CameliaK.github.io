Level of students' competence

The students are able to apply discourse analysis techniques in a foreign language inside and outside the language classroom through the understanding, comparison, and discussion of different discursive elements in both oral and written discourse. In this course, the students show a sense of responsibility, commitment, respect, and honesty to identify forms that promote language learning and enhance their teaching practice.

Description

This course introduces students to the study of discourse analysis, with a focus on how language is used in real-world communication. It explores key concepts such as cohesion, coherence, spoken vs. written discourse, genres, discourse types, and the applications of all these into teaching English as a foreign language. Students will analyze authentic texts and interactions by means of theoretical discussions and practical applications, which in turn will enhance their ability to teach and assess language use effectively.

Carlos Núñez Mercado

Universidad Veracruzana

Head of the online BA in ELT Department since 2019, teaches at the BA in English Language and the online BA in ELT of the Universidad Veracruzana in Mexico. He holds an MA in TEFL and a PhD in Language Studies and Applied Linguistics, both from the Universidad Veracruzana. He has over 10 years of experience teaching EFL students in both high school and higher education levels. He also holds a C2 level language certification from the University of Cambridge. He has participated in the online BA in ELT COAPEHUM certifications and redesign committees.

He has supervised numerous students’ research projects from the BA and MA programmes of the university; his research interests and publications focus on teaching linguistic and communicative skills, the use of ICT in ELT, and Corpus Linguistics methods in language studies. He is a member of the Research Group Applied Linguistics in English Language Teaching and Learning (UV-CA-543). He has presented a number of papers at national and international conferences.

Daniel Jacobo Gidi Martí

Universidad Veracruzana

BA in English Language Teaching, MA in Teaching English as a Foreign Language and PhD in Educational Research from the Universidad Veracruzana. He has taught for more than 10 years in public and private educational institutions. He obtained his first scientific publication in the journal Teaching Innovations of the University of Belgrade, Serbia. His main line of research is student-centred English language learning and he belongs to the Research Group "Linguistics and Translation" of the Universidad Veracruzana.

Patricia Núñez Mercado

Universidad Veracruzana

Holds a BA in English, a BA in French, a TEFL MA from the Universidad Veracruzana, and a PhD in Applied Linguistics from the University of Southampton. She is a full-time lecturer at the School of Languages of the Universidad Veracruzana, teaching at the BA in English, the online BA in TEFL, the TEFL MA, and the Foreign Language Department. She was the coordinator of the English academy from 2005 to 2011, the head of the English BA Department from 2011 to 2013, and the coordinator of the tutorial system of the same BA programme from 2013 to 2015. She has supervised numerous students’ research projects in all the programmes she teaches.

She belonged to the Standardized Exams committee of the English BA from 2002 to 2012, and the EXAVER designers’ committee in 2015. She has also taught teacher training courses and workshops for the School of Languages academic staff and has participated in the certification and redesign committees of the BA and MA programmes. She holds a C2 language certification from the University of Cambridge and was a member of the CENEVAL committee for the BA in ELT certification exam. She coordinates the Research Group Applied Linguistics in English Language Teaching and Learning (UV-CA-543) and is a member of the RECALE (a national network for research groups in foreign languages). Her research interests are L2 literacy in higher education, English language learning and teaching, and research processes. She has participated in numerous national and international conferences, both as a presenter and attendee. She has published several book chapters and journal articles, coordinated several books on the ELT training area, and co-authored a research report writing handbook for beginners.
