TEYL Forum A. Introduction forum  

Instructions:

  1. Welcome to the TEYL course. In this forum, you'll have to share with the group:
    1. Your name.
    2. English interests.
    3. The reasons why you took this learning experience.
    4. Expectations you have got from this course.
    5. Drop by to say hello to at least ONE of your classmates.
  2. Thank you for your commitment and group work!

TEYL Forum B. Krashen’s hypotheses  

Score: 5 %

Instructions:

  1. Read the document titled Krashen five hyphoteses. PDF file
  2. In a single paragraph, state the differences between language learning and language acquisition according to Krashen (1994).
  3. Briefly discuss Krashen's five hypotheses. Try to argue their potential suitability for your teaching context.
  4. Give critical thinking related feedback to at least ONE of your classmates.
  5. Download the rubric.

TEYL Activity 01. Observing rituals / routines  

Score: 10 %

Instructions:

  1. Go to a real classroom other than yours.
    • Ask a colleague for letting you observe their classroom and their children by using a consent form.
    • Design your own consent form for asking for permission to observe and add it to your task, with the corresponding signature, as an appendix.
  2. After arranging a date to observe, download the file and fill in the blanks with insighful information that covers the sociocultural theory of Bruner.
    3. TEYL Activity 01Word document
  3. At the bottom of the sheet, you need to collect the signature and full name of the teacher your observe. Follow your ethical and professional principles for doing this task.
  4. Download the rubric.
    5. Rubric Word document
  5. Once you finish this activity, save and name it TEYL01_NameSurname, and upload it in Eminus in the corresponding section in ‘Actividades’.

TEYL Forum C. Piaget’s active learning  

Score: 5 %

Instructions:

According to Cameron (2010, p. 2), 'it is through taking action that learning occurs'. After reading Cameron's paper (pp. 2-5):

  1. Write and share a concrete definition of ACTIVE LEARNING.
  2. Plus, provide a detailed example of how children may learn in your language classroom through EXPLORATION; state clearly how assimmilation and accommodation might take place in your example.
  3. Finally, read and comment on at least two classmates' definitions of ACTIVE LEARNING and their examples of learning through exploration; courteously comment on whether you agree, desagree or simply give your feedback.
  4. Download the rubric.

TEYL Forum D. Vygotsky’s social constructivism  

Score: 5 %

Instructions:

According to Vygotsky, children will learn throughsocial interaction (with people), eventually internalising the use of language. Internalised language is that language available to a child to use without having to go through the step of mentally translating.

  1. Participate in the forum by establishing what internalisation is (you might read Cameron again, or any other source of information, but make sure you give proper credit).
  2. Revise Cameron's reading again (2001, pp. 5-8) and write your own definition of what the Zone of Proximal Development ZPD (is p. 3). Plus, succinctly define the role you play as a teacher in enhancing children's ZPD (p. 4).
  3. Finally, give feedback to at least one of your classmates.
  4. Download the rubric.
